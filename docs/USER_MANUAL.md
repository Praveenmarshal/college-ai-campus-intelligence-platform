# User Manual — Students & Faculty

## Getting Started

1. Go to the platform URL and click **Create one** on the login page to register, or use credentials provided by your institution.
2. After logging in you'll land on your **Dashboard**, showing campus-wide stats and quick links.

## AI Chat

The **AI Chat** page (sidebar → AI Chat) is the fastest way to get answers:

- Ask about uploaded documents: *"What's the attendance policy?"*
- Ask about your data: *"What's my CGPA?"*
- Ask about placements, library books, hostel rooms, events — anything covered by
  the platform's 9 specialised agents.

The assistant automatically figures out which part of the system to check (a PDF,
a database table, an ML model) — you don't need to pick a mode. Every answer that
draws on an uploaded document shows source citations as chips below the response.

Your conversations are saved under **Chat History** and can be revisited or deleted.

## My Profile

- **Profile** page: update your name, phone, and department; change your password.
- **Settings** page: toggle dark/light mode and notification preferences.

## Student-Specific Features

- **Student Portal**: your CGPA, attendance %, AI-predicted attendance/CGPA trend, and fee status at a glance.
- **Resume Analyzer**: upload your resume (PDF) and optionally a target job role.
  You'll get an ATS compatibility score, strengths/weaknesses, skill gap analysis,
  and concrete improvement suggestions — all generated locally, nothing leaves
  campus infrastructure.
- **Attendance / Placements / Academic Analytics**: read-only dashboards with
  charts for the whole institution (your own numbers are in Student Portal).
- **Library / Hostel / Events**: ask natural-language questions ("Is *Clean Code*
  available?", "Any vacancy in Block A?", "What events are coming up?").

## Faculty-Specific Features

- **Faculty Portal**: your subjects and profile details.
- **Attendance Analytics**: department trends and an **at-risk students** list
  (anyone below 75% attendance) so you can intervene early.
- All student-facing analytics pages are also available to you.

## Question Paper Analyzer

Upload a past question paper (PDF or photographed/scanned image) to extract:
topics covered, difficulty level, question types, and count. Useful for exam
preparation or spotting recurring themes across years.

## Tips

- If a chat answer seems generic, it likely means no relevant document has been
  uploaded yet for that topic — ask an admin to upload it via **Admin → Upload PDF**.
- Dark mode persists across sessions (stored locally in your browser).
- All AI responses are generated by a local model and may occasionally be wrong —
  always verify anything high-stakes (e.g. exact fee amounts, deadlines) against
  the official source.
